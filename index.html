Value #1: Stop Development

It's time for the City to accept that this is our island. I want it to be in good shape when my kids grow up.

Currently it seems like every last inch of the island is either in development or being slated for future development. When will it end? Are we going to wait until there isn't any open space left?

Value #2: Taking the Next Steps

If we are going to advance the Town’s interests, we need a municipal code of laws and law enforcement.

If we want to save trees and make our power grid hurricane resistant, we need underground power lines and modern power distribution.

Value #3: Transparency From The Town

I plan to keep my council Facebook page updated with live-feed and minutes from council meetings so you can stay informed. I’d also like to have the emailed meeting notices go out earlier so residents who are interested in the agenda have more time to make plans to attend.

Value #4: Public Safety

My neighbors have too frequently posted home security footage of their cars and front doors being tested or broken into by thieves. It’s persistent and we need to put a stop to it. I’ve sent a FOIA to the Charleston County Sheriffs Office for all reported crimes in the last 6 months for zip code 29412. I had the results for larceny, vehicle break-ins, and other theft and home security crimes plotted on a map, which you can find here:

As a trial lawyer, I am acquainted with how the judicial system works. James Island needs the system to work for its tax-paying residents, and I plan to deliver that service to our doorsteps.

About Me

After graduating the College of Charleston in 2004, I worked as a cook aboard the SCDNR's Lady Lisa. Following that field season, I worked for NOAA out of the Pacific Marine Center, which back then was in Seattle, WA. My job was to operate the sonar equipment used to map the seafloor in order to update nautical charts and study marine habitat. I worked in very remote locations in the pristine wilderness of Alaska, off the densely populated coast of New England, throughout the Caribbean, and for a few months out of Honolulu. As shipmates, everyone on board had to work together to accomplish our surveys safely and efficiently. It was an influential experience and helped shape the person I am today.
